Understanding Why Users Ignore Data Breach Warnings

This project studies how users react to data breach notifications and why many people ignore security warnings even after being alerted.

Problem Statement

Many users receive data breach alerts but do not take action such as changing passwords or enabling security features. This increases their risk of account compromise. The project aims to understand the behavioral reasons behind this inaction.

Literature Review

Research studies indicate that users experience "warning fatigue" and often ignore repeated security alerts. Human-computer interaction research shows that message clarity, urgency, and design influence how seriously users take security warnings.

Research Gap

Most existing systems focus on detecting breaches but do not analyze how users respond after being notified. This project focuses on understanding user behavior at the student level.

Methodology

Data Collection

An anonymous survey will be conducted among students to measure awareness level, response behavior, and password habits.

Analysis Method

Responses will be categorized based on awareness, action taken, and risk perception. Basic statistical analysis and graphical representation will be used.
